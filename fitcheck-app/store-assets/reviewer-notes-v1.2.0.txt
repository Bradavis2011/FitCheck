STEP-BY-STEP WALKTHROUGH
================================================================================

STEP 1 — SIGN IN
  • Open the app. You will see the Or This? welcome screen.
  • Tap "Sign In" (not "Create Account").
  • Enter: reviewer@orthis.app / Review2026!
  • No email verification is required for this pre-existing account.

STEP 2 — HOME SCREEN
  • After sign-in you will land on the Home tab.
  • The home screen shows a hero prompt and a "Check My Outfit" CTA button.
  • Recent outfit history cards appear below (pre-seeded for the demo account).
  • Tap "Check My Outfit" or the elevated Camera tab (center of tab bar) to begin.

STEP 3 — CAMERA / PHOTO SELECTION
  • Tap the camera shutter to take a photo, or tap the gallery icon to pick one
    from your photo library.
  • On first use, the app will request Camera permission and/or Photo Library
    permission via the standard iOS/Android system dialog.
  • These permissions are used solely to capture or select the outfit photo.
    No photos are stored on our servers.

STEP 4 — AI CONSENT MODAL (first outfit check only)
  • Before the first outfit check, a one-time disclosure modal appears.
  • The modal states: "This app uses Google Gemini AI to analyze your outfit.
    Your photo is processed in real time and is not stored by Google."
  • The reviewer must tap "I Understand" to proceed. This consent is recorded
    and the modal does not appear again for this account.
  • If you wish to see the modal again, sign out and use the app for the first
    time with a fresh account.

STEP 5 — CONTEXT INPUT
  • After selecting a photo, the app presents a context screen.
  • Fill in optional fields: Occasion, Setting, Weather, Vibe, Concerns.
  • These fields help the AI tailor its feedback. All fields are optional.
  • Tap "Get Feedback" to submit.

STEP 6 — AI FEEDBACK SCREEN
  • The app displays an outfit score (1–10), a section of what is working,
    a section of things to consider, and quick-fix suggestions.
  • Scores ≥ 8 appear in green, ≥ 6 in amber, and < 6 in red.
  • You can tap the share icon to generate a shareable score card image.

STEP 7 — OUTFIT REVISION SYSTEM (NEW in v1.2.0)
  • On the feedback screen, a "Revise Outfit" button appears below the feedback.
  • Tap it to enter the Revision Flow:
      1. The app presents targeted swap suggestions based on the AI feedback
         (e.g., "Try a white button-down instead of the graphic tee").
      2. You can upload a new photo of the revised outfit.
      3. The AI compares the original and revised look and provides a
         before/after score delta, noting specific improvements.
  • This feature is the primary new addition in v1.2.0.
  • Free accounts may use up to 1 revision per outfit check.
    Plus accounts have unlimited revisions.

STEP 8 — ARCHIVE (HISTORY)
  • Tap the Archive tab to view past outfit checks.
  • Filter chips at the top let you filter by All, Favorites, or occasion type.
  • Tap any card to view its full feedback and revision history.

STEP 9 — PROFILE
  • Tap the Profile tab to view account details, subscription status, and
    usage stats (checks today / daily limit).
  • The demo account has Plus status; the daily check limit shown is 10.

================================================================================
PERMISSIONS SUMMARY
================================================================================

Camera
  Requested on first tap of the camera shutter.
  Used to capture an outfit photo. Not used in background.

Photo Library
  Requested when the user taps the gallery picker icon.
  Used to select an existing photo. Not used in background.

Notifications (Push)
  Requested automatically after sign-in (iOS) or on first launch (Android).
  Used for optional style nudges (e.g., "What are you wearing today?").
  The user can deny this permission without any loss of core functionality.

================================================================================
ADVERTISING
================================================================================

Or This? uses non-personalized ads only.

The app sets requestNonPersonalizedAdsOnly: true in all ad network
configurations. No App Tracking Transparency (ATT) prompt is shown because
the app does not track users across apps or websites owned by other companies.
There is no cross-app behavioral targeting or IDFA usage.

================================================================================
PRIVACY & DATA HANDLING
================================================================================

Photos
  Outfit photos are sent to Google Gemini API for real-time analysis.
  Photos are NOT stored by Google after processing. The AI response (text
  feedback) is stored on our servers (Railway/PostgreSQL) and associated with
  the user's account for history purposes. Users can delete their account and
  all associated data from Profile → Delete Account.

Personal Data
  Email address collected at registration (via Clerk authentication).
  No financial data is stored by Or This? — payments handled by RevenueCat/Stripe.

AI Disclosure
  Users receive a one-time in-app disclosure modal before their first outfit
  check, explaining that Google Gemini processes their photo. This satisfies
  the requirement to disclose AI/ML data processing to users.

================================================================================
KNOWN LIMITATIONS / REVIEWER TIPS
================================================================================

• If the AI feedback screen shows a loading spinner for more than 10 seconds,
  the Gemini API may be rate-limited. Tap "Try Again" to retry.

• The demo account has pre-seeded outfit history. If you want to test a fresh
  first-time experience, you may create a new account with any email address
  (email verification required for new accounts).

• Outfit photos taken during review will be stored in the demo account's
  history. They will not be visible to other users (outfits are private by
  default).

• The Revision System (v1.2.0 feature) requires a completed outfit check first.
  Use any photo — stock photos of clothing work fine.
