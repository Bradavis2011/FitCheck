# Or This? — Pitch Deck Speaker Notes

Use these notes when presenting the deck. Each section corresponds to a slide. For YC interviews, aim for the full deck in under 3 minutes. For investor meetings, expand to 8-10 minutes.

---

## Slide 1: Title

> "Or This? is the first agentic platform for fashion. Not an AI feedback app — a platform that accumulates personalized style intelligence, reaches users proactively before moments that matter, and converts that intelligence into commerce."

*Don't linger here. Move to the problem immediately.*

---

## Slide 2: The Problem

> "Every morning, millions of women stand in front of the mirror and ask 'does this actually look good?' Outfit indecision is one of the most universally reported daily stressors for women — and every existing solution fails at the moment it matters.
>
> Friends are biased. Partners say 'you look fine.' Social media is too slow and too public. Pinterest gives inspiration but not feedback on what you're actually wearing. A stylist costs $100 to $500 and they're not available at 7:45am on a Tuesday.
>
> But the deeper problem isn't the single moment of decision. It's that no tool learns from you between sessions, works for you while you're not looking, or connects style intelligence to what you actually buy. That's the platform gap."

---

## Slide 3: The Solution — Three Pillars

> "Or This? solves this in three layers.
>
> First: Accumulating Intelligence. Every outfit check builds a personalized style profile — Style DNA. The AI learns your color season, your body type, your fashion goals, your occasion patterns. Feedback gets sharper the more you use it. The app knows you.
>
> Second: Proactive Outreach. 16 server-side agents run continuously without the user opening the app. Weather alerts before a meeting. Event follow-ups after a date night. Milestone messages. Push nudges timed to each user's peak engagement hour. The platform works for users while they sleep.
>
> Third: Intelligence-to-Commerce. When the AI identifies a color story that's working, it surfaces affiliated product recommendations calibrated to that verdict. Style intelligence converts to curated shopping — zero ad spend, commission on conversions.
>
> This is not a tool you open. It's a platform that works for you."

---

## Slide 4: The Agentic Architecture

> "Under the hood, this is a fashion-specialized AI running inside an agentic infrastructure — think SoHo stylist who charges $400 an hour, operating at software scale.
>
> The AI delivers verdicts, not suggestions. Score first, explanation second. Specific. Decisive. No hedging.
>
> The agentic layer is what makes this a platform. 16+ autonomous backend agents handle content creation, growth monitoring, user engagement, email sequences, affiliate sourcing, app store management, and weekly founder briefings. There's a security model — executeOrQueue — that gates high-risk actions for human review while auto-executing low-risk ones. One founder operates this at the scale of a 10-person team.
>
> The AI self-calibrates. When helpfulness drops, the automated critic pipeline finds the prompt weakness and corrects it. Quality improves overnight without a new release."

## Slide 6: Business Model

> "Three revenue layers.
>
> Consumer subscriptions: Free users get 3 verdicts per day. Plus at $7.99 unlocks unlimited verdicts and community features. Pro at $14.99 adds expert reviews and style analytics.
>
> Affiliate commerce: When the AI identifies a gap or a winning color story, it surfaces curated product recommendations via CJ Affiliate and Rakuten. Commission on conversions — no ad spend required. The intelligence that makes users trust the verdict is the same intelligence that makes product recommendations convert.
>
> Fashion intelligence B2B: At scale, every outfit analyzed generates structured data — what real people actually wear to real occasions, scored by AI and validated by community. Trend reports, real-time API, white-label SDK. Think WGSN's $100M+ ARR, but built on actual consumer behavior rather than runway predictions."

## Slide 7: Unit Economics

>

>
> Affiliate commerce adds a high-margin revenue layer with no incremental AI cost — the same intelligence that drives the verdict drives the conversion."

Data depth compounds — the moat widens.

## YC Interview Tips

**For the 10-minute YC interview:**
- Lead with the platform framing, not the app framing (30 seconds)
- Demo the product live if possible — show the verdict reveal (60 seconds)
- Explain why now / why you / why agentic (60 seconds)
- Let them drive the rest with questions

**Common YC questions to prepare for:**
1. "What do you understand that others don't?"
2. "Who are your first 100 users and how do you get them?"
3. "What's your biggest concern about this business?"
4. "How does this become a billion-dollar company?"
5. "If we fund you, what do you do in the first 2 weeks?"
6. "What have users told you that surprised you?"
7. "Why hasn't someone done this already?" (Answer: agentic infrastructure cost a team 18 months ago; fashion AI quality threshold just crossed)
8. "How do you handle the cold start?" (Answer: AI solves it — no community needed to start; Style DNA builds from day one)

**Key talking points to hit:**
- "First agentic platform for fashion" — the category frame, not the feature frame
- "Three pillars" — Accumulating Intelligence, Proactive Outreach, Intelligence-to-Commerce
- "Works while you're not looking" — the platform differentiator in one phrase
- "16 agents, one founder" — the leverage story
- "Margins improve automatically" — AI cost deflation
- "Built, not pitched" — the agentic infrastructure is operational code, not architecture diagrams
